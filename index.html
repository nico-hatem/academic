[Website in progress] Welcome! I am a Postdoctoral researcher at CentraleSupelec working on issues in environmental economics.

My research is on climate change mitigation policies, especially for reducing the carbon intensity of energy. I am particularly interested in the deployment of low-carbon energy supply technologies, such as solar photovoltaic installations, and its efficiency given the (possible) decentralisation of energy infrastructures.

I have recently acquired a PhD from the Paris School of Economics (December 2024).

In Progress
  • Ground-mounted solar and the impact of land-use planning: evidence from France. Abstract: This paper provides novel evidence on how land-use regulation impacts the deployment of solar photovoltaic installations in France. I investigate a joint-regulation setting. Solar energy projects must satisfy eligibility criteria to participate in renewable energy auctions, determined by the land‐use designation of the proposed site. Eligibility criteria, in turn, are prescribed using land‐use planning frameworks at the municipality level. Using an event study, I investigate how this interaction impacts the amount of land allocated to solar at the local level. My findings suggest that the fragmentation and heterogeneity of land-use planning across jurisdictions may distort the spatial deployment of solar. Municipalities with more detailed land-use planning increase the amount of land allocated to solar by an average of 1,000 m² per km² of land eligible for new developments, due to better alignment with national regulation. Conversely, more recent land-use planning and frameworks integrated at the inter-municipality level reduce the amount of land by -1,500 $m^2$ per $km^2$, due to stricter land conservation objectives.
